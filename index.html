Welcome to Brady Texas

  • 1870 - The Brady community was settled in the 1870s and was named Brady City, after the Brady Creek which runs through town.
  • 1876 - Brady grew quickly after it was chosen as the seat of McCulloch County on May 15, 1876.
  • 1877 - In 1877, Brady had a population of about 50 residents.
  • 1878 - The County couthouse was built from native sandstone in 1878.
  • 1884 - By 1884, Brady had two churches, one district school, three stores, two hotels, and more than 300 residents.
  • 1880s - 1890s - The number of farms and fences greatly increased by the influx of immigrants. Poultry, sheep, goats, cotton, and pecans became the important sources of income for the local residents.
  • 1903 - In 1903, the Fort Worth and the Rio Grande Railway arrived, Brady become a major shipping point for Central Texas.
  • 1906 - The name was shortened to Brady when the town was incorporated in 1906.
  • 1910 - The old, three-story McCulloch Jail was built, and served as a correctional facility for many years. Inside the jail is a “death cell” that was used for extremely troubled prisoners.
  • 1920s - 1950s - The Brady population grow slowly from the 1920s through the 1950s, with the highest population being 6,800 in 1958.
  • 1974 - The jail became to a historical museum and was named the Heart of Texas Historical Museum.
  • 1974 - 16 teams got together to roast some goat in an effort to raise money for the Jaycees of McCulloch County, becoming the first Goat Cook Off.
  • 1988 - By 1988 Brady had 5925 residents and 142 businesses.
  • 1989 - Pari-mutuel wagering returned to Texas after 52-year hiatus at G.Rollie White Downs.
